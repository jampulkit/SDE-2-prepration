# Interview Intro, Closing & Negotiation

## 1. "Tell Me About Yourself" (2 minutes)

**Structure: Present → Past → Future. Keep it under 2 minutes.**

```
Template for a 4-year payments/fintech engineer:

"I'm a backend engineer with 4 years of experience, currently working at [Company]
in the payments/fintech space. I work on [specific system], which processes
[X transactions/day] and handles [specific responsibility].

In my current role, I've led [1-2 key achievements]:
- [Achievement 1: e.g., led migration from monolith to microservices, reducing outages by 90%]
- [Achievement 2: e.g., designed the idempotency framework for our payment processing pipeline]

My tech stack is Java, Spring Boot, Kafka, Redis, and AWS. I'm particularly strong in
distributed systems and building reliable payment infrastructure.

I'm looking for my next role because I want to [genuine reason: work on larger scale problems,
grow into a senior/staff role, work on a product I'm passionate about]. That's what drew me
to [Company] — [specific reason about the company]."
```

**Rules:**
- No life story. Start from your current role.
- Mention 1-2 quantified achievements.
- End with why you're interested in THIS company.
- Practice until it flows naturally (not memorized-sounding).

## 2. "Why This Company?"

| Company | What to Say | What NOT to Say |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| **Amazon** | "I want to work at a company that operates at massive scale. Amazon's leadership principles, especially Ownership and Dive Deep, align with how I work. The payments/fintech space at Amazon is particularly exciting because of [specific: Amazon Pay, AWS billing]." | "I want the brand name" or "Good salary" |
| **Google** | "I admire Google's engineering culture and the emphasis on solving hard problems at scale. I'm excited about [specific team/product]. The opportunity to work with world-class engineers and contribute to products used by billions is compelling." | Generic praise without specifics |
| **Meta** | "I'm drawn to Meta's 'move fast' culture. Building products that connect billions of people is meaningful to me. I'm particularly interested in [specific: payments infrastructure, commerce platform]." | Anything about the metaverse unless you genuinely care |
| **Flipkart** | "Flipkart is solving unique challenges for the Indian market at massive scale. My payments experience directly applies to [specific: payment processing, seller payouts]. I want to build products that impact hundreds of millions of Indian users." | "It's a stepping stone to a US company" |

## 3. "Why Are You Leaving?"

**Safe framing (never badmouth current employer):**

| Reason | How to Frame It |
|--------|----------------|
| Want more growth | "I've learned a lot in my current role, and I'm looking for opportunities to take on larger scope and grow into a senior engineering role." |
| Want different domain | "I want to apply my backend skills to [new domain]. My payments experience gives me a strong foundation for [target area]." |
| Want more scale | "I want to work on systems that serve hundreds of millions of users. My current system handles [X], and I'm ready for the next level of scale." |
| Layoffs/reorg | "My team was restructured, and I see this as an opportunity to find a role that's an even better fit for my skills and career goals." |

## 4. Questions to Ask the Interviewer

**Ask 2-3 questions per round. Tailor to the interviewer's role.**

### For Hiring Manager
1. "What does success look like for this role in the first 6 months?"
2. "What are the biggest technical challenges the team is facing right now?"
3. "How do you balance feature development with technical debt?"

### For Peer Engineer
1. "What does a typical day look like on the team?"
2. "What's the most interesting technical problem you've worked on recently?"
3. "How does the team handle on-call and incident response?"

### For Skip-Level / Senior Leader
1. "What's the team's roadmap for the next year?"
2. "How does this team's work fit into the company's broader strategy?"
3. "What qualities do the most successful engineers on this team share?"

### Questions to Avoid
- "What does the company do?" (shows no research)
- "How soon can I get promoted?" (premature)
- "What's the salary?" (save for recruiter/HR)
- "How many hours do people work?" (signals low motivation)

## 5. Salary Negotiation Basics

**When to discuss:**
- Recruiter asks early: "What are your salary expectations?"
  → Deflect: "I'd like to understand the full scope of the role first. I'm sure we can find a number that works for both of us."
- After receiving an offer: this is when you negotiate.

**How to negotiate:**
1. Express enthusiasm: "I'm excited about this opportunity."
2. Ask for time: "Can I have 2-3 days to review the full package?"
3. Research market rate: levels.fyi, Glassdoor, Blind
4. Counter with data: "Based on my research and experience, I was expecting [X]. Can we discuss?"
5. Negotiate total comp, not just base: stock, signing bonus, relocation, level

**Competing offers:**
- Having multiple offers is the strongest negotiation lever
- "I have another offer at [X level]. I prefer your company because [reason], but the compensation gap is significant. Can we close that gap?"
- Never lie about offers. Recruiters talk to each other.

**What's negotiable:**

| Component | Negotiable? | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| Base salary | Sometimes (bands are often fixed) | Easier at smaller companies |
| Stock/RSU | Yes (especially at FAANG) | Ask for more units or accelerated vesting |
| Signing bonus | Yes | One-time, easier for company to approve |
| Level | Sometimes | If you're borderline, push for higher level |
| Start date | Yes | More time = more prep for next role |
| Relocation | Yes | If applicable |

## 6. Revision Checklist

- [ ] "Tell me about yourself": 2 min, Present → Past → Future, end with "why this company"
- [ ] "Why this company?": specific reason tied to the company's product/culture/scale
- [ ] "Why leaving?": positive framing, never badmouth current employer
- [ ] Questions for interviewer: 2-3 per round, tailored to their role
- [ ] Salary: deflect early, negotiate after offer, use competing offers as leverage
- [ ] Negotiate total comp (base + stock + bonus), not just base salary
